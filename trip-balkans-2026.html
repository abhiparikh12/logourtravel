The trip at a glance

Good to know

Before you go

🚗 Driving & borders

  • A self-drive trip; the longest days are Day 8 (Dhërmi→Shkodër, ~409 km) and Day 11 (Shkodër→Mostar, which crosses Montenegro and Bosnia).
  • Confirm your rental is cleared for cross-border travel into Bosnia & Montenegro before you leave the desk.
  • Download offline maps for all three countries; mountain coverage is patchy (Theth pass, southern Albania).
  • Keep passports and car papers within reach on the cross-border days.

🥗 Eating well (vegetarian-friendly)

  • Southern Albanian and Montenegrin Konoba menus are rich in dairy, produce and rice — vegetarians eat very well.
  • For garden and farm-to-table dinners, call ahead to confirm group size and dietary needs.
  • Carry cash: many rural spots and boat operators are cash-only. Euro is widely accepted; Albania uses the lek, Bosnia the marka.

☀️ July / August in the Balkans

  • Most beaches are pebbles, not sand — pack water shoes.
  • Beat heat & crowds: reach Butrint, Kotor Old Town and mountain summits at opening.
  • Pack repellent (Butrint), grippy shoes (thermal baths, Gjipe, Theth) and a light layer (Lipa Cave sits at 8–12°C).
  • Sunsets run ~8:00–8:10 PM — the hilltop castle dinners are timed for golden hour.
